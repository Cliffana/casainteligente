Energy Monitoring — The Differentiator

The Tapo P115 stands out for including energy consumption monitoring — it shows how many kWh each device uses, in real time and with history. Great for identifying wasteful appliances (old freezers, heaters, etc.).

Shelly also offers monitoring, but the app is less intuitive. The Amazon Smart Plug does not have this functionality.

Ease of Installation

They are all simple: - Tapo P115 and Amazon Smart Plug: open app, add device, connect to WiFi. 2 minutes. - Shelly Plug S: requires the Shelly Cloud app, which is a little more technical. For advanced users it is excellent, for beginners it can be confusing.

Tap P115 — Pros
- Energy monitoring included - Excellent price (~12€) - Intuitive app - Compact (does not block neighboring sockets) - Compatible with Alexa and Google Home
Tap P115 — Cons
- Requires Tapo app (does not work with Alexa alone) - WiFi 2.4GHz only
Final Verdict
The **Tapo P115** is the clear winner. It costs less than €12, has energy monitoring that no competitor offers at this price, and the app is easy to use. For Home Assistant users, Shelly is more flexible. The Amazon Smart Plug only pays off if you want to stay exclusively in the Alexa ecosystem without installing extra apps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a hub to use smart plugs?
No. All outlets in this comparison connect directly to WiFi. Just install the app and connect.
Can I control the outlet when I'm away from home?
Yes. All three brands allow remote control via app, as long as the phone has internet and the socket is connected to WiFi.
How much do I save on my electricity bill?
Monitoring sockets (Tap P115) show consumption in real time. You can program automatic shutdown of standby devices. Savings depend on usage, but €5-10/month is realistic.
