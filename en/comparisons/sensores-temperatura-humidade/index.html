Temperature and Humidity Sensors for Smart Homes — Which is Best?

TL;DR
**Aqara**: Zigbee sensor, requires a hub, excellent accuracy, 2 year battery. Ideal for users with the Xiaomi/Home Assistant ecosystem. **SwitchBot**: compact, with integrated LCD display, rich app. Ideal for use alone or with the SwitchBot ecosystem. **Govee**: Direct, cheap WiFi, alerts on your cell phone. Ideal for beginners without a hub. **Verdict:** Aqara for reliability. SwitchBot for display on the sensor. Govee for tight budget.

SwitchBot has the best accuracy and the integrated LCD screen is very practical — you can see the temperature without opening the phone. Aqara is the king of autonomy (2 years with a CR2032 battery). Govee is the cheapest and doesn't need a hub.

Aqara — Pros
- Excellent reliability (never fails) - 2 year battery - Zigbee — mesh network, does not overload WiFi - Seamless integration with Home Assistant and Xiaomi - Small and discreet
Aqara — Cons
- Need Zigbee hub (or Aqara gateway) - No display (only in the app) - Initial setup requires some technical knowledge
Final Verdict
**Aqara** is the best for reliability and integration with Home Assistant/Xiaomi ecosystem. If you already have a Zigbee hub, there's no better choice. **SwitchBot** is the best for everyday use — the integrated display is super practical for seeing the temperature at a glance. **Govee** is the best for beginners: cheap, direct WiFi, no hassle. Recommendations: - 1 sensor per main room (bedroom, living room, kitchen) - 1 sensor outside (balcony/garden) if the model can handle it - Connect sensors to automation: turn off heater above 22°C, turn on dehumidifier above 70%

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Zigbee and WiFi sensor?
Zigbee sensors (like Aqara) need a hub but consume less battery (last ~2 years). WiFi sensors (like Govee) connect directly to the router but use more battery and are less stable with many devices.
Does the sensor work with Home Assistant?
Yes. Aqara is the best for Home Assistant (via Zigbee2MQTT or ZHA). The SwitchBot works with their hub. Govee works with official cloud integration.
How much does it cost to maintain a sensor?
Practically nothing. The Aqara battery lasts 2 years (costs ~1€ to replace). The Govee uses 2 AAA batteries and lasts ~6-12 months. The SwitchBot uses 1 CR2450 and lasts ~6 months.
