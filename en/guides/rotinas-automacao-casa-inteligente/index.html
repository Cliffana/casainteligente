What are Automation Routines?

An automation routine is a rule like: "If X happens, then do Y". Examples:

  • "If the front door opens after 8pm, the entrance light turns on"
  • "If the temperature exceeds 25°C, turn on the fan"
  • "If you say 'Alexa, good morning', it turns on the lights and says the weather"

Routines are what transform a collection of smart devices into a truly smart home — one that reacts on its own, without needing commands.

Key Points
- Routines = trigger + action - Work by voice, time, sensors or location - Start simple: 1 trigger, 1 action - Always test before trusting

5 Essential Automations to Get Started

1. Automatic Lights with Motion Sensor

The most useful and easy-to-do automation. Need: - 1 motion sensor (Aqara, ~12€ AliExpress) - 1 smart bulb (Tapo L530E, ~14€)

Rule: If the sensor detects movement → turns on the light. After 2 minutes without movement → goes out.

Ideal for hallways, bathrooms and entrances. Never look for the switch in the dark again.

2. Open Door Alert

Rule: If the door/window opens → sends notification on the cell phone.

You need an Aqara door sensor (~8€). Useful to know if the house door was left open, or if the bedroom window opened during the night.

3. Morning Routine by Voice

Rule: "Alexa, good morning" → turns on lights at 50%, reads the news, says the weather.

No extra sensors needed. All you need is an Alexa or Google Home and a smart light bulb. In 2 minutes it's done.

4. Temperature Control

Rule: If the temperature exceeds 25°C → turn on the fan/smart AC. If it drops below 18°C ​​→ turn on the heater.

You need a temperature sensor (~10€) and a smart socket (~12€). The Tapo P115 has energy monitoring — you can see how much each device uses.

5. Presence Simulation

Rule: Between 7pm and 11pm, turn lights on and off randomly in various rooms.

It gives the impression that there are people at home even when they are on vacation. All you need is smart bulbs — you can program them using the Alexa or Tapo app in 5 minutes.

How to Create Routines on Alexa

  1. Open the Alexa app
  2. Go to More → Routines
  3. Click on the + (top right corner)
  4. In "When", choose the trigger: - Voice (ex: "Alexa, cinema") - Schedule (e.g. "Every day at 7am") - Device (e.g. "Motion sensor detects") - Location (e.g. "When you get home")
  5. Under "Action", choose what happens: - Turn lights on/off - Connect socket - Send notification - Say something
  6. Click Save

How to Create Routines on Google Home

The process is identical: 1. Open the Google Home app 2. Go to Automations → Add 3. Choose the trigger and action 4. Save

Pros and Cons of Automations

Benefits
- Comfort — lights that turn on by themselves - Security — open door alerts - Savings — appliances turn off automatically - Accessibility — useful for people with reduced mobility
Care
- Don't automate critical things (locks without physical backup) - Test for 1 week before trusting - Always have a manual plan B (physical switch) - Unstable WiFi may fail automations

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a hub to create automations?
Not necessarily. If you use WiFi devices (Tapo, Sonoff, Govee), automations work directly through apps or Alexa/Google Home. Zigbee sensors (Aqara) need the respective hub but bring more advanced routines.
Do the routines work if the internet fails?
It depends. Routines in the cloud (Alexa, Google Home) require internet. Local routines (Home Assistant, Aqara Hub) work offline. For critical automation (security), prefer solutions with local processing.
How many routines can I have?
On Alexa and Google Home the limit is very high (hundreds of routines). In practice, 5-10 well-done routines cover 90% of the needs of a typical home.
